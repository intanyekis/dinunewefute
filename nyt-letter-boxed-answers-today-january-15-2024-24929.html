NYT Letter Boxed

Letter Boxed is a word puzzle game created by The New York Times. In Letter Boxed, you need to make words using letters arranged in a box. The catch is that each word must have at least one letter from each side of the box. As you go through the game's levels, the box's sides increase, making the puzzles harder. There are more than 200 levels, and they get tougher as you progress.

The game has a timer, which means you must create as many words as you can before time runs out. This gets you points and unlocks higher levels. Additionally, there are daily puzzles that let you earn extra points if you solve them quickly.

How to Play NYT Letter Boxed?

  • First start the game by finding the first word, then click on any of the letters.
  • Now choose another letter, the Letter Boxed game will draw a line between them
  • You cannot select a letter on the same side of the box, after you form the word.
  • You need to cross one of the three other sides.
  • If you wish you can make use of the same letter multiple times in a word.
  • When you find each word, the next word must begin with the last letter of the preceding word.
  • The words should be at least 2 letters long.
