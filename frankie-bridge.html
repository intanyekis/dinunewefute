Frankie Bridge is an English singer, songwriter, dancer, and model who is a former member of the pop group S Club 8 and a member of the girl group The Saturdays, alongside Una Healy, Rochelle Humes, Mollie King, and Vanessa White. The Saturdays was formed in 2007 and has released several albums, tracks, and extended plays like Chasing Lights, On Your Radar, Living for the Weekend, Wordshaker, Headlines!, Chasing the Saturdays, If This Is Love, Ego, Up, Just Can’t Get Enough, Issues, Work, My Heart Takes Over, 30 Days, Gentleman, What Are You Waiting For?, Missing You, All Fired Up, Higher (ft. Flo Rida), What About Us (ft. Sean Paul), and Disco Love. In addition to that, Frankie Bridge has also been a part of several projects such as Viva S Club, Hollyoaks Later, I Dream, Ministry of Mayhem, Smile, Loose Women, I’m a Celebrity…Get Me Out of Here!, Something for the Weekend, Impulse into Glamour Presents: A Diary of the Saturdays T4 Special, The Door, Cannonball, Strictly Come Dancing, Ant & Dec’s Saturday Night Takeaway, The Xtra Factor, Myth, Big Brother’s Little Brother, and Never Mind the Buzzcocks.
