How did Aleksander Emelianenko start his Professional Career?

Aleksander frequently stayed with his brother because his parents worked all day. Fedor, his brother, was a Sambo practitioner at the time. Aleksander’s brother accompanied him so that he would not miss any of his practices. As a result, Emelianenko began practicing martial arts at a young age. Initially, he observed older children practicing and soon picked up the moves and began mimicking them.

In addition, during elementary school, Aleksander began formal Sambo training with Vladimir Mihailovich Voronov. Aside from Sambo, Aleksander practiced wrestling, judo, and boxing, as well as football and basketball. Furthermore, Aleksander’s mother forbade him from practicing boxing. He did, however, practice martial arts in secret.

Aleksander became Judo’s Russian Master of Sport when he was 16 years old. He also won the European Sambo championships the same year he graduated. In addition, he won the World Combat Sambo championships in 2002, 2003, and 2006. Similarly, in 2010, he represented the city of Saint Petersburg in the Russian Cup of Combat Sambo. And he won the tournament’s heavyweight division.

Following his victory, Aleksander earned the right to represent Russia at the World Championships. He competed in the Sport accord Combat Games 2010 in Beijing after winning the Russian Cup of Combat Sambo. In addition, the Russian fighter took silver in the +100 kg category.

During an interview, he said, “his punches are like electric trains, and if he misses, his opponents will catch a cold.”

Furthermore, he won matches until his fourth, when he was defeated by a Croatian MMA fighter named Mirko Cro Cop.
