The summer transfer window will close TONIGHT as Premier League clubs scramble to get some last-minute deals done.

The 2022 transfer windows were record-breaking in the top-flight as a total of £1.9billion was spent in the summer.

That figure has already been smashed time summer with the Premier League's top clubs spending big once again.

Chelsea broke the British transfer record to sign Moises Caicedo while Arsenal spent over £100million on Declan Rice and Man United forked out £72m for Rasmus Hojlund.

There should be plenty more transfer activity right up until the deadline as clubs prepare their squads for the busy season ahead.

When is deadline day?

The Premier League have confirmed that clubs will have until Friday, September 1 to make their signings.

The window will close at 11pm on Friday with deadline day deals expected to go down to the wire.

The EFL deadline will be the same as the top-flight while the window will shut at midnight in Scotland.

The transfer windows across Europe will also slam shut today.

The Bundesliga market will close at 5pm, Serie A at 7pm, Ligue 1 at 10pm and La Liga at 11pm.
